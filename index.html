Judge Blake Sylvia

A career guided by fairness, compassion, and service to the community.

Early Foundations in Law

After earning his law degree from the Sandra Day O’Connor College of Law at Arizona State University, Judge Sylvia began his legal career in Phoenix, Arizona. From the start, he distinguished himself by translating complex legal issues into clear, practical solutions that empowered clients and advanced just outcomes.

Desert Justice Chambers: A Practice Built on Integrity

As his reputation grew, Judge Sylvia founded Desert Justice Chambers, a practice devoted to fairness, transparency, and results. He focused on Family and Property Law, representing clients in custody disputes, divorce proceedings, and land-rights litigation. Clients often said that working with him never felt like working with a “typical lawyer”—a reflection of his approachable style and deep empathy.

From Counselor to Judge

After decades of service, Judge Sylvia’s commitment to justice and ethical leadership led to his appointment to the bench. As a judge, he carries forward the same principles that guided his practice: fairness, balance, and compassion—listening carefully, weighing every fact, and issuing rulings that reflect both the letter of the law and the humanity behind it.

Service Beyond the Courtroom

Judge Sylvia’s contributions extend beyond his judicial duties. He mentors young attorneys and speaks on access to justice, legal ethics, and entrepreneurship in law, while volunteering time to protect land rights and prevent unlawful evictions.

A Balanced Life

Outside the courtroom, he finds balance in family, nature, and lifelong learning—hiking Arizona’s trails, cooking for loved ones, traveling to explore diverse cultures, and reading biographies that inspire reflection on leadership and resilience.

A Legacy of Fairness and Service

From his first day as a lawyer to his current role on the bench, Judge Blake Sylvia has lived by a guiding principle: justice is about people, not just cases. His career stands as a testament to integrity, empathy, and the belief that the law, when applied with wisdom, can uplift lives and strengthen communities.
